Short Autobiography

Age 1

Before the age of 2, there isn't anything much I can talk about. It's mostly really negative experiences which I won't share. Other than that I managed to survive a feral cat bite that happened when I was in crib. I started education at just 1.5 by going to a childcare until admissions opened.

Age 2

At the age of 2, I ran away from a school before taking admission into pre-nursery at another school.

Age 3

At the age of 3, I had to do pre-nursery again in another school, which ate my "early bird advantage" and dragged me down to those who started at "normal age". Learned writing in English, Hindi, Punjabi, sorted in order of proficiency that time.

Learned about technology including installing third party apps, Bluetooth, web browsing etc, through a Symbian device. Collected stones a bit but later stopped in favor of plants. Survived brain trauma caused by accidental fall to head, and I had no role in it.

Age 4

At age of 4, I did my first shallow water swim. Expanded my knowledge of technology, using only J2ME phones and slow internet connection. I utilized various methods including overnight, bulk downloads and keeping device in open area. Discovered the basic concept of elasticity, magnetism through "playing" with magnets, bouncing balls, which I had bought myself, with my own money, rubber bands etc.

Started teaching myself English. I learned it through many sources including newspapers, books, writing, reading a dictionary, watching non-fiction television channels including History TV 18, Discovery, Animal Planet, Nat Geo etc.

Age 5

At the age of 5, I started collecting coins, notes and got my first gold medal in overall academics, by scoring highest, which was also first possible gold medal as before that, there were no awards.

Discovered astrology, horoscope in a newspaper. Initially thought it a "Book for predicting future", due to credibility of newspapers, but rejected it due to lack of accuracy, impossibility of predicting future for such large amount of people with limited number of signs, and vague statements.

Almost amputated my finger while trying to open an electronic with a knife. I still have wound of this.

Age 6

At the age of 6, I got another gold medal, gave my first public speech at school, was forced to play a role into school annual function and continued coin collection. I also started collecting APKs, after learning the process of backing, editing up them, which was very helpful to me due to low storage, and internet speeds.

Started climbing trees and practicing jumping. It should be noted that I did not jump out of sake of it, but for a practical necessity, such as travel, retrieving ball, playing hide and seek etc. I employed proper techniques such as hanging to reduce distance. I also did not try rolling or back flip, despite an unsolicited advice of a self proclaimed expert, because I knew a roll technique he was teaching could break the neck, which is way worse than breaking limbs; my jumps weren't really that high which warranted a roll; possibility of pebbles on ground.

Age 7

Served as discipline manager for whole academic session.

I learned about firecraft including firepit, boiling, roasting plant material, pottery, blacksmithing(didn't practice due to improper equipment), realizing that dry leaves aren't really dry. Started long distance cycling(total 10Km + a day), despite being underweight(BMI < 13)

Age 8

At the age of 8, I started teaching myself basic theoretical electricity. I wrote my own note titled “How to Make Electric Formulas”. Convinced my parents to stop sending me to tuitions which led to increase in grades, freedom, decrease in costs. I haven't taken any tuition since then.

Age 9

At the age of 9, I started focusing more on stationery, software side of technology, learned about modding Android applications to remove ads, in-app purchases. Wrote a mathematical table reference notebook. Won a mental maths quiz and an English poem recitation competition in school.

Started volunteering by counting, curating donations at a temple, where my father held voluntary managerial position.

Age 10

By the age of 10, I had shaped my interest in finance, technology, felids/nature, stationery collection. I had negative experience with all of them but that didn't stop me. Rather they had an opposite effect.

I started taking my stationery collection seriously. I also introduced this hobby, trading, written communication using obscure words, tabletop games, hidden pockets and bills to my classmates. I had collected 227 sharpeners, over 150 pens through both trading, purchasing and deterring thieves on the basis of maths teacher.

Maths teacher considered me her "favorite student", gave me “Smart and Cool :smile:” on official report card, forced me to eat my lunch in front of her, and also gave keys to her personal locker, but I never used it for personal storage. I was the only student who sat with opposite gender. She forced me to sit with one girl for a week, who was not very intelligent and came from a lower socioeconomic class in efforts to teach her. Until I told her that it is "impacting my studies".

I also played the front role in school annual function, again forcefully admitted, but had privilege of being only student excluded from makeup(skin whitening powder), because I am already pale, and wearing my own jacket(because according to teachers, including of maths, it looked good).

Also scored 96/100 in environmental science, A1 in oral exams of all 3 languages, of mathematics, despite being forced to take Sanskrit, instead of Punjabi, till age 13, due to lack of choice by school.
