About Me

By the age of 10, I had shaped my interest in finance, technology and felids/nature. I had negative experience with all three of them but that didn't stop me. Rather they had an opposite effect. I collected coins by buying it at higher than face value from beggars, going to shady places. I almost amputated my finger when trying to open an electronic device with a knife. I still have wound of this. I also managed to survive a feral cat bite who bited me when I was in crib, and other injuries caused while "playing" in nature.

I was around 12 year old when I began teaching myself basics of Linux, Python and cybersecurity. I used Termux because I didn't have a computer. I learned these technologies purely out of curiosity, but soon, I started using them for my day to day computing needs and for automating repetitive tasks.

After learning it for about one and a half year, I created my own group for computer, cybersecurity related discussions, that gained over 100 members, and I started learning about finance, stocks, cryptocurrency. I made a profit of around $13 in cryptocurrency, but as I was a minor at that time, I was not able to withdraw it. So I started focusing more on programming, trading equities in demo account.

At the age of 15, I created two groups. One on Telegram for computer and cryptocurrency discussion that gained over 150 members. Another one on a private web chat platform. Made my first pull request in open source project, started learning about internet privacy and security. After gaining enough knowledge about it, I began taking steps to protect myself including deleting all my identities, groups, turning off JavaScript, using a VPN.

At the age of 16, I convinced my parent to open an equity trading account for me, and I started investing my savings into low expense-ratio ETfs and few individual stocks. In less than 6 months, I was able to make a profit of around 10%. I also uploaded my first project, a password manager, at this age on Github.

Age of 17 was a transitory period whose reward I received by the age of 18. I learned about lot of things, made many decisions including making list of over 120 universities to avoid, what exams to take, what programming career should to pursue, how should to handle legal matters after turning 18 and more. Despite that, I bought a custom built laptop, and customized it further. Created a group on Matrix, that gained over 200 members, and released related scripts. Released Awesome J2ME, which gained over 50 stars. Gained intermediate knowledge of nutrition, C++ and released DietCLI.

At the age of 18, I started my professional life as "HstSethi". Created this website, online accounts, projects, resume and an an educational, tech group, on the first day of college. Stopped being a "Jack of all trades, but master of none" and started focusing on specific set of skills including AI engineering, technical writing and finance. Volunteered as a tester as Github Models Limited Public beta. I also reached 25% profit in equity, over 70 stars on Github, over 650 followers on Linkedin, completed Hacktoberfest 2024 with 6 pull requests accepted, including 4 in top 5 highest starred projects. Got 865/~8000 rank in IPU CET BCA 2024 without any kind of reservations or formal education.

That was just a start. I strongly believe in self directed[^2], lifelong learning. I am continuously learning new things, learning from my previous mistakes. I do not solely focus on technology and finance. I also do have knowledge of various other topics including philosophy(epistemological skepticism, naturism, asceticism(as a means to end, not the end in itself), agnostic pragmatism, existentialism, political philosophy), psychology, wilderness, nutrition, business, history, culture(especially my own, Punjabi).

Things I dislike are: anything fictional including art, video games, music, movies, novels. Consumerism(foodism, pet culture, modern web, ...)

For more information regarding my finance journey, checkout my free financial autobiography From Coins To 25% In Equity At 18: My Financial Autobiography. In it I share, how a coin collection hobby paved my way as an equity trader, making 25% profit in equity at the age of 18, on his own.

For more information regarding my online leadership journey and groups I have created, check out my free memoir 5 Lessons From Founding Tech Groups + Timeline.
